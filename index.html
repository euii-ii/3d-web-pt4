QUEEN BEE
01
Biological Facts

Only One Per Colony – A honeybee colony typically has only one queen at a time. If a new queen emerges, she either fights the old one or the colony swarms. Lifespan – A queen bee can live up to 5 years, much longer than worker bees (who live only a few weeks to months). Egg-Laying Machine – She lays up to 2,000 eggs per day, ensuring the survival of the colony. Fed Royal Jelly – Queen bees develop from larvae that are fed exclusively royal jelly, a nutrient-rich secretion from worker bees. Mating Ritual – A queen mates with multiple drones during a single mating flight, storing sperm to fertilize eggs for the rest of her life.

02
Role in the Colony

Pheromone Control – She releases pheromones to keep the colony organized and suppress other female bees from becoming fertile. Not a Dictator – While she is essential, the worker bees make many decisions (like when to swarm or replace her). Can Be Replaced – If the queen becomes weak, old, or stops laying eggs, the worker bees will raise a new queen by feeding a larva royal jelly.
